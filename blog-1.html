5 Things Every Small Business Website Needs in 2026

5 min read  ·  By ScriptaForge

Most small business websites we review are missing at least two of these five things. And it's costing them enquiries every single day. Here's what actually moves the needle when it comes to turning website visitors into paying customers.

1. A headline that says exactly what you do

Within three seconds of landing on your site, a visitor should know: who you are, what you do, and who you do it for. That's it. Most business owners write headlines like "Welcome to our website" or vague slogans that say nothing. Instead, try something like: "Custom kitchens for London homeowners — designed and fitted in 6 weeks." Clear beats clever every time.

2. A single, obvious call-to-action above the fold

Don't make visitors hunt for how to contact you. Your primary call-to-action — "Get a Quote", "Book a Call", "Request a Callback" — should be visible without scrolling, on every device. One clear button. Not three. Decision fatigue is real, and too many options means no action is taken.

3. Mobile-first design

Over 60% of web traffic now comes from mobile devices. Yet most websites are designed on a desktop and then "made to work" on mobile as an afterthought. If your site is hard to read, slow to load, or has buttons too small to tap on a phone — you're losing more than half your potential customers before they even read a word.

  • Text should be at least 16px on mobile
  • Buttons should be at least 44px tall (easy to tap)
  • No horizontal scrolling — ever
  • Images should be compressed for fast mobile loading

4. A load time under 3 seconds

Google research shows that 53% of mobile visitors leave a page that takes more than 3 seconds to load. A beautiful website that loads slowly is a beautiful website nobody sees. Compress your images, use modern hosting, and avoid bulky page-builder plugins. Speed is not optional — it directly affects both your Google rankings and your conversion rate.

5. Real social proof

People trust people, not businesses. Adding genuine reviews, testimonials, and client logos to your website is one of the highest-impact changes you can make with zero design work. Even two or three honest reviews from real customers will dramatically increase the number of people who reach out. Ask every happy client for a testimonial — most will say yes.
